## Deleterious Mutation Burden and Genetic Load Analysis
To characterize the distribution of deleterious mutations, we analyzed 100-kb sliding windows (containing at least one SNP and excluding selective sweep regions) using the dataset `DMRef_100KSNP.window_AllDeleBurden...`.
1. **Comparative Burden:** We quantified the reduction in deleterious burden in the *Candolleanum* (CND) population compared to elite lines (A626 and E463), identifying windows where the minimum burden across CND accessions was lower than that in the elite cultivars.
2. **Sample Contribution Analysis:** To identify which CND accessions contribute most significantly to the observed genetic load, we applied a **greedy selection algorithm**. Starting with a baseline column (accession), the algorithm iteratively selected up to 50 additional samples ($k=2$ to $51$) that maximized the reduction of the summed minimum burden across all genomic windows.
3. **Output:** The analysis generated detailed logs and summary statistics (CSV and TXT formats) tracking the incremental reduction in genetic load at each step of the selection process, highlighting the core accessions responsible for the majority of the deleterious variant burden.
